Best Chocolate countries

Intro

Chocolate is one of the popular food in the world. Chocolate has been around about for 4,000 years. Archeologies has found that, it was first produced by pre-Olmec cultures living in present-day Mexico. Then in 1500s, Spanish conquistador Hernán Cortés who sought gold and silver in Mexico returned instead with chocolate. After as time passed the popularity of chocolate increased. Now, chocolate is known world wide. You can fine chocolate just about anywhere. But, there are only few countries that are well known for its production and lavish taste of chocolate.

  • Switzerland

    Chocolate production began in Switzerland in the 17th century. Until WWII, swiss chocolate industry was heavily export oriented. Today switzerland is the largest consumers of chocolate within their own country. They are also the highest chocolate consumers in the world. which is nearly 30 pounds per capita every year. Chocolate is also the primary source of wealth for the country.some of the World-renowned chocolate brands that originated in Switzerland include Nestle, Toblerone, Lindt.

  • Belgium

    Belgium is world-renowned for its chocolates, and it is a major chocolate manufacturing center. Since 1884, the composition of Belgian chocolate has been regulated by law. To ensure the purity of the chocolate. Belgium's annual sales of chocolate is approximately $12 billion. One of the reason why Belgium chocolate is well known because, they are mostly produced by hand without any modern equipment. World-renowned chocolate brands that originated in Belgium are Godiva and Wittamer.

  • North America

    North America is one of top, high quality chocolate producer. Most corporations engaged in the manufacture of chocolate in the U.S. and purchase their cocoa beans from the Ivory Coast in Western Africa. North America’s annual sales of chocolate is approximately $12 billion.The largest chocolate company in North America, and one of the most recognized chocolate brands worldwide, is the Hershey Foods Corporation, more commonly known as Hershey’s.
